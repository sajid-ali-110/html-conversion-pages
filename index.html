NINTEDANIB IN PROGRESSIVE FIBROSING
INTERSTITIAL LUNG DISEASES (ILDs) 1

Trial design

Clinical ILD diagnoses

Hypersensitivity pneumonitis
Unclassifiable IIP
Autoimmune ILDs
Idiopathic NSIP
Other fibrosing ILDs

Rationale for nintedanib in progressive fibrosing ILDs

  • There are no approved therapies for progressive fibrosing ILDs other
    than IPF
  • There are clinical and mechanistic similarities among progressive
    fibrosing ILDs
  • Nintedanib slows disease progression in IPF 2 and decline in
    pulmonary function in systemic sclerosis associated ILD 3

Aim of the INBUILD® trial

  • To investigate the efficacy and safety of nintedanib in a broad range ofn
    patients with fibrosing ILDs with a progressive phenotype other than IPF

Primary endpoints

Annual rate of decline in FVC (mL/year) assessed over 52 weeks in:

  • the overall population
  • patients with UIP-like fibrotic pattern on HRCT

Nintedanib reduced the annual rate of decline
in FVC compared with placebo

  • Time to first acute exacerbation or death over 52 weeks
